LATTICE — Open-Source Accountability Protocol

A framework-agnostic Python library providing content-addressed (SHA-256), cryptographically signed (Ed25519) accountability for multi-agent AI systems. Every agent decision is recorded as a Claim in a directed acyclic graph, enabling backward traceability from conclusions to raw evidence.

Alignment with Prof. Siwik's Research

Multi-agent autonomy under constraints. LATTICE directly addresses the challenge of trusted autonomous decision-making in environments where human oversight is limited or delayed — a core requirement for space-exploration systems and mission-critical autonomous architectures.

Cyber-resilient, secure-by-design systems. LATTICE's cryptographic integrity layer (content addressing + Ed25519 signatures) and revocation waterfall mechanism provide tamper evidence and compromise propagation tracking — capabilities essential for dual-use and mission-critical systems.

Swarm and distributed autonomy. The framework-agnostic accountability protocol generalizes to any multi-agent topology, including swarm architectures where individual agents must make independent decisions while maintaining collective verifiability.

Onboard reasoning under constraints. LATTICE's offline-first, zero-dependency architecture (SQLite + minimal Python) is designed for constrained deployment environments — directly applicable to onboard reasoning systems with limited bandwidth and computational resources.

Doctoral Research Direction

I aim to extend LATTICE into a formal accountability and verification framework for autonomous multi-agent systems operating in mission-critical, bandwidth-constrained environments — bridging the gap between practical deployability and formal guarantees for AI-driven space and security systems.
